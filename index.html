👤 About

Mamunur Rahaman is a researcher at the intersection of biomedical science and artificial intelligence, developing computational methods for healthcare with a focus on multimodal medical AI and computational pathology. He completed his PhD in Computer Science and Engineering at UNSW Sydney (January 2026), supervised by Professor Erik Meijering, Professor Anant Madabhushi, and Associate Professor Ewan Millar.

His research spans deep learning for histopathology image analysis, spatial transcriptomics–guided modeling, and vision–language approaches for data-efficient and zero-shot learning. Across these directions, his goal is to build robust, clinically meaningful models for diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment stratification by integrating histopathology with complementary modalities, including radiology, molecular and clinical data, and multimodal textual knowledge.

Mamunur has contributed to over 40 peer-reviewed journal and conference papers, with an H-index of 25 and over 3,500 citations on Google Scholar. His work has received international recognition, including four ESI Highly Cited Papers (top 1% in the Engineering field) and a Scientific Reports publication ranked among the top 100 cancer research papers of 2023. In 2025, he was honored by ScholarGPS as a Highly Ranked Scholar (Prior Five Years) in the specialty of Histopathology, placing in the top 0.05% of scholars worldwide for productivity, impact, and quality of scholarly work.

He held a Visiting Fellowship at the Wallace H. Coulter Department of Biomedical Engineering at Georgia Institute of Technology and Emory University (Dec 2024–Apr 2025), collaborating with Professor Anant Madabhushi’s group on advanced AI methods for computational pathology in cancer research. He also served as a Visiting Researcher at the Technical University of Munich (Oct 2025), where he delivered a seminar on multimodal pathology AI and engaged with the Schüffler Lab. He recently served as a Postdoctoral Writing Fellow at UNSW (Nov 2025–Jan 2026), supporting research writing and publication outcomes.

Mamunur is currently a Research Fellow at the Australian Institute of Health Innovation (AIHI), Macquarie University (March 2026–Present), where he leads AI-driven research in computational pathology and spatial transcriptomics for precision oncology.

He earned a BSc in Electrical and Electronic Engineering (High Distinction) from BRAC University, Bangladesh, and an MSc in Biomedical Engineering (Highest Distinction) from Northeastern University, China. At UNSW, he tutored courses including Computer Vision (COMP9517), Neural Networks and Deep Learning (COMP9444), and Artificial Intelligence (COMP9814) (May 2022–Jan 2026). He contributes to the research community through editorial roles (including Associate Editor at Frontiers in Oncology—Breast Cancer and Executive Editor at AI Medicine) and extensive peer-review service across over 20 journals, including IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging, IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics, Scientific Reports, and Computers in Biology and Medicine.
