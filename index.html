Md Mamunur Rahaman

Postdoctoral Researcher · University of New South Wales, Sydney, Australia

✉ Email 🎓 Google Scholar 💻 GitHub 🔗 LinkedIn 🏛 UNSW Profile

I am a researcher working at the intersection of biomedical science and artificial intelligence, developing AI models and computational methods for healthcare. My work focuses on multimodal medical AI (histopathology, radiology, molecular, and clinical data) and building robust, clinically meaningful models for diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment stratification.

I recently completed my PhD in Computer Science and Engineering (January 2026) at the School of Computer Science and Engineering, University of New South Wales (UNSW), Sydney, Australia, with a thesis titled Advancing Computational Pathology with Multimodal Deep Learning. My PhD advisors were Prof. Dr. Erik Meijering, Prof. Dr. Anant Madabhushi, and A/Prof. Dr. Ewan Millar.

I have contributed to over 40 peer-reviewed journal and conference papers, achieving an H-index of 23 and accumulating over 3,400 citations on Google Scholar. Four of my publications are recognized as ESI Highly Cited Papers (top 1% in Engineering), and my 2023 Scientific Reports paper was ranked among the top 100 cancer research papers of 2023.

I am a DAAD AInet Fellow (Postdoc-NeT-AI – AI for Science, 2024) and was a Visiting Fellow at the Madabhushi Lab, Emory University & Georgia Tech (2024–2025), and a Visiting Researcher at the Schüffler Lab (AI for Pathology), TU Munich (2025). I received the Arc PGC Research Candidate Award (2025) and the UNSW University International Postgraduate Award (2022–2025).

My research addresses key clinical problems—including cancer prognosis, treatment stratification, and biomarker discovery—using cutting-edge deep learning, vision-language models, and spatial transcriptomics. I am currently leading projects on spatial transcriptomics-guided computational pathology for prostate cancer (validated on CHAARTED and RTOG 0521 clinical trials) and multimodal AI biomarkers for Head and Neck Squamous Cell Carcinoma.
