👤 About

Md Mamunur Rahaman is a Research Fellow at the Australian Institute of Health Innovation (AIHI), Macquarie University, where he works at the intersection of artificial intelligence, computational pathology, and translational biomedical research. His research focuses on developing clinically relevant multimodal AI methods that integrate histopathology with molecular, clinical, and other complementary data sources to improve cancer diagnosis, prognostic assessment, and treatment stratification.

He completed his PhD in Computer Science and Engineering at UNSW Sydney in January 2026, supervised by Professor Erik Meijering, Professor Anant Madabhushi, and Associate Professor Ewan Millar. His work spans deep learning for histopathology image analysis, spatial transcriptomics-guided modeling, vision-language learning, and multimodal foundation-model approaches for data-efficient and zero-shot medical AI.

He has authored over 40 peer-reviewed journal and conference papers, with an H-index of 25 and more than 3,500 citations on Google Scholar. His work has received international recognition, including four ESI Highly Cited Papers and a Scientific Reports publication ranked among the top 100 cancer research papers of 2023. In 2025, he was recognised by ScholarGPS as a Highly Ranked Scholar (Prior Five Years) in Histopathology, placing in the top 0.05% of scholars worldwide for productivity, impact, and quality of scholarly work.

His academic appointments and research visits include a Visiting Fellowship at the Wallace H. Coulter Department of Biomedical Engineering at Georgia Institute of Technology and Emory University (Dec 2024–Apr 2025), a Visiting Researcher appointment at the Technical University of Munich (Oct 2025), and a Postdoctoral Writing Fellowship at UNSW Sydney (Nov 2025–Jan 2026). These roles supported collaborations in computational pathology, multimodal AI, and translational cancer research.

In his current role at Macquarie University, he is advancing AI-driven research in computational pathology, spatial transcriptomics, and precision oncology. His ongoing and upcoming research also includes work on motor neurone disease (MND) involving gene data, extending his translational AI research toward data-driven neurological disease analysis.

He also works as casual academic staff at UNSW Sydney, tutoring courses in the School of Computer Science and Engineering since 2022, including Computer Vision (COMP9517), Neural Networks and Deep Learning (COMP9444), and Artificial Intelligence (COMP9814). More broadly, he contributes to the research community through editorial service, topic-editing activities, and peer review for leading journals in medical imaging, biomedical AI, and computational pathology.

He earned a BSc in Electrical and Electronic Engineering (High Distinction) from BRAC University, Bangladesh, and an MSc in Biomedical Engineering (Highest Distinction) from Northeastern University, China.
